My Alizeh,

I don't even know how to start this. I've typed and deleted the first line like four times now. Maybe that's fitting though, because what I feel for you has never been something I could just put into words easily.

I keep thinking back to how all of this began, and it almost feels ridiculous now — how something that started so casually could end up meaning this much. In the beginning, we were too relaxed about it all. We took each other for granted in ways I don't think either of us fully understood at the time. We were just two people talking, drifting, existing in each other's orbit without realizing how deeply we were already becoming part of one another's lives. We didn't know then that something so ordinary could quietly turn into something so important.

We met on Tango, of all places — somewhere trust is fragile, where people come and go, where most conversations disappear as quickly as they start. And somehow, in that space, we found each other. I think that alone says a lot. Because in a place where it would have been so easy to stay guarded, we kept opening up a little more each day. We didn't trust easily at first — I don't think either of us did — but slowly, almost without noticing, we started trusting each other more and more. That trust didn't come all at once. It was built piece by piece, through late conversations, through consistency, through the strange kind of comfort that only comes when someone keeps showing up.

And then there was that long gap. The year and a half that changed everything. The gap where we lost contact completely, and life carried us in different directions. That time hurt in ways I still don't think I know how to explain properly. There's something especially hard about losing someone who once felt so close and then having the silence stretch out for months, then longer, until it starts to feel like the world has quietly decided to move on without you. I know things happened, and I know life was messy in that time in ways I don't need to spell out. But what I do know is that I never really stopped feeling that absence. Even when I acted nonchalant and behaved like I had moved on, I never really stopped missing you and what we had.

And then somehow, you came back into my life last year, and everything that I had buried so carefully came rushing back all at once. I don't think either of us fully expected that. But I think a part of me always knew that what was between us had never really gone away. It had just gone quiet for a while.

This year has not been easy either. January and February were hard — really hard. We barely talked, and the distance between us felt sharper than it had in a long time. I remember feeling like we were standing right at the edge of collapsing again, and I hated that feeling. I hated how close we came to losing each other all over again. There were moments when I wasn't sure what would happen next, moments when everything felt fragile and one wrong word or one wrong move could break something my heart never wanted to lose. That kind of distance hurts differently when it's someone you love. It doesn't feel empty — it feels awfully heavy.

And still, even through all of that, we fought and found our way back.

That's the part I keep coming back to. No matter how much time passed, no matter how many wrong turns, no matter how many times life tried to separate us, we always came back to each other in some way. Maybe not perfectly. Maybe not cleanly. But always honestly.

And now here we are — after all these years, all these delays, all the plans that got cancelled for one reason or another, all the times we said "soon" and had to watch that soon become later and later — we're finally meeting next month. I still don't think my mind has fully caught up to that fact. It feels so unreal to me that after all this time, after talking across distance for so long, after carrying each other through screens and time zones and missed chances, I'll finally get to see you in front of me. Actually see you. Actually stand there with you. Actually turn this thing that has lived so long in words and calls and feelings into something real I can hold in my hands.

Maybe that's why this means so much to me. Because we didn't get the easy version of this. We got the toughest version — the one that asked for patience, for trust, for restraint, for faith, even when things were uncertain. We got the version that had to survive distance, circumstance, and time. And somehow, against all odds, it did.

I know I've said this countless times (or maybe still not enough?), but you are one of the rarest things in my life. Not because everything has been perfect — it hasn't. Not because we've never hurt each other — we have. Not because we've always known exactly what to say — we haven't. But because through all of it, you stayed the most important soul in my life. You kept mattering. Even in the weeks where we barely spoke. Even in the months where I didn't know where we stood. You were always the love of my life.

And if I'm being honest, I think I've loved you most in the moments when I had to quietly come to your live and just watch you chirp away like you always do. Those little stolen moments where I could just sit there and see you being you, where I didn't need to say anything at all, and somehow that felt more intimate than anything else. The ones where just seeing you, even from a distance, felt like enough.

So no, this isn't me trying to write the perfect love letter. It isn't polished enough for that. It's just me trying — writing this on another long flight to our future home in New York — as honestly as I can, to tell you what you mean to me before we finally meet next month and everything changes into something I've been waiting for for far too long.

I love you, Alizeh Shireen.

Not just in the easy parts.
Not just when everything feels soft and certain.
